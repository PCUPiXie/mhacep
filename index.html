Know the Signs & Build Resilience

Common Signs to Look For

Changes in behavior, feelings, or thinking that persist for several weeks or more may signal a mental health challenge:

  • Significant changes in appetite or sleep patterns.
  • Loss of interest or pleasure in usual activities.
  • Feeling consistently low, sad, or hopeless.
  • Unexplained fatigue or low energy.
  • Difficulty concentrating or remembering things.
  • Increased irritability or short temper.
Immediate Coping Techniques

When feeling overwhelmed, try these simple, grounding exercises to quickly calm your nervous system:

  • **The 5-4-3-2-1 Method:** Name 5 things you can see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, and 1 you can taste.
  • **Deep Breathing:** Inhale slowly for 4 seconds, hold for 4 seconds, exhale slowly for 6 seconds. Repeat 5 times.
  • **Mindful Movement:** Take a short, intentional walk, focusing only on the sensation of your feet hitting the ground.
  • **Journaling:** Write down everything you are feeling without judgment for five minutes.
